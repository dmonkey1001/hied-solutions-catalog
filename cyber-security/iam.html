Identity & Access Management (IAM)

Ensuring the right individuals have the right access to the right resources.

← Back to Cyber Security

Identity and Access Management (IAM) is a foundational element of cybersecurity, focused on managing digital identities and controlling their access to systems and data. IAM solutions help governments enforce the principle of least privilege, ensuring that employees and citizens only have access to the information necessary for their roles. This reduces the risk of data breaches caused by compromised credentials or insider threats.

Key Features

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Adding a critical layer of security by requiring a second form of verification beyond just a password to access applications.

Single Sign-On (SSO)

Improving user experience and security by allowing employees to log in once to access all their authorized applications.

Access Governance

Automated processes for requesting, approving, and recertifying user access to ensure permissions are always appropriate.

Privileged Access Management (PAM)

Securing, managing, and monitoring access for highly privileged accounts (e.g., system administrators) to prevent misuse.
