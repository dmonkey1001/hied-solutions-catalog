Internet of Things (IoT)

Connecting the physical world to create smarter, more responsive communities.

← Back to Emerging Technologies

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical objects—from streetlights to water meters—embedded with sensors and software that connect and exchange data over the internet. For governments, IoT enables the creation of "smart cities" where real-time data from the physical environment is used to improve services, reduce costs, and enhance quality of life for residents.

Example Solutions

Intelligent Traffic Management

Connected traffic signals that adapt to real-time conditions to reduce congestion and improve traffic flow.

Smart Utility Metering

Automated water and energy meters that provide real-time usage data and automatically detect leaks or outages.

Environmental Monitoring

Sensors deployed in public spaces to monitor air quality, water quality, and noise levels to support public health initiatives.

Smart Waste Management

Sensors in public trash bins that notify collection crews when they are full, optimizing routes and reducing fuel costs.
